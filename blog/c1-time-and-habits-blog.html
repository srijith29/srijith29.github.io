Time and Habit

I have tried some of the habit and time management techniques. From the materials I have gained some new insights as well. I have meditated and have been exposed to different types from different schools (one of the perks of being from India). I have experienced peace, tranquility and above all happiness during my practises. The biggest challenge for me in continuting with any of those practises has been reaching a state where I begin to see futility in achievements and it stops me from going after a bucket list or that comfortable life. I might have to change my perspective from an achievement mode to a self discovery one.

I am currently planning to do the promodoro technique as a way to keep myself focussed for short bursts. I particularly like the fact that its doable as it requires focus for 25 minutes. The other challenge would be managing the breaks as I tend to wander off for hours during breaks. I think is important to have quality breaks not quantity.

The video on habit formation has given me some great ideas on how to create a small habit and small habits over a period makes larger one. I like the blog where it says starting small is very important and confidence is a result of doing. The technique of making new habits by breaking it into very small manageabale chunks and repeating it just after an established routine or habit is one thing I will start experimenting with.
